Note: Connecticut and New Mexico still have inmates on death row despite having abolished the death penalty. The death penalty in these states was only abolished for future crimes. All data is provided from the Death Penalty Information Center.

One of the most common arguments in favor of the death penalty is that it serves as a deterrent for capital crimes such as rape and murder. The following four-year data represents the trends in the murder rates within each state. If the argument mentioned above were true, one should see that states imposing the death penalty have lower murder rates than states that have abolished the death penalty (sample size may affect results). Note: A state's murder rate is obtained by dividing the number of reported murders by a state's total population.
